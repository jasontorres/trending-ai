Building a civic tech movement isn't just about writing code—it's about creating infrastructure for democracy itself. BetterGov.ph started as a simple idea: make government data readable and actionable for citizens. Today, with over 3,600 members, we've built budget dashboards, anomaly detection systems, emergency response tools, and blockchain-based transparency platforms.

This is the story of how we scaled from a weekend project to a nationwide movement, the technical challenges we faced, and the lessons learned about building technology that serves the public good. From navigating government bureaucracy to designing systems that citizens actually use, here's what 10+ years of civic tech taught me about the intersection of democracy, transparency, and code.
