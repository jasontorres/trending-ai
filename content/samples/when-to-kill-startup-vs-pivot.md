Founders face an impossible question: is this a temporary setback or a fundamental flaw? Do we pivot or do we shut down? I've been on both sides of this decision multiple times. One pivot saved a dying company and led to an acquisition. Another pivot delayed the inevitable and wasted six months of our lives. The difference wasn't obvious in the moment—but looking back, the signals were there.

This essay explores how to make the kill-or-pivot decision: identifying the difference between execution problems (fixable) and market problems (often not), recognizing when you're pivoting toward opportunity vs. away from failure, the sunk cost fallacy in startup form, and how to have honest conversations with co-founders about when it's time to move on. Shutting down a startup is painful, but it's less painful than slowly dying while pretending you're pivoting.
