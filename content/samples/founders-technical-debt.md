Every technical founder faces the same dilemma: build it right or build it fast? The answer is almost always "build it fast"—until it isn't. The trick is knowing when to shift gears, when that scrappy MVP code becomes a liability, and how to pay down technical debt without killing momentum. I've rebuilt the same product three times across different companies. Each time, I learned something new about when to optimize for speed vs. sustainability.

This essay explores technical debt as a financial instrument: something you take on deliberately with a plan to pay it back. We'll look at the warning signs that your debt is compounding dangerously, strategies for incremental refactoring without stopping the ship, and how to have honest conversations with non-technical co-founders about why "just add the feature" isn't always possible anymore.
