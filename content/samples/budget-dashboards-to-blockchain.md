Our first transparency tool was embarrassingly simple: a budget dashboard that turned government PDFs into interactive charts. Citizens could finally see where their taxes went. A decade later, we're running blockchain-based systems for immutable government records and ML-powered procurement monitoring. This evolution wasn't planned—it emerged from understanding what actually drives accountability.

This article traces the journey from simple data visualization to sophisticated transparency infrastructure. We'll explore what worked, what failed spectacularly, and the surprising lessons about when high-tech solutions matter and when a well-designed spreadsheet is all you need. Technology doesn't automatically create accountability, but the right tools at the right time can shift power dynamics in meaningful ways.
