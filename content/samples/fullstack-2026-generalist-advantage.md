The industry keeps predicting the death of the full-stack engineer. "You can't be an expert at everything," they say. They're right—but that's not the point. The value of being full-stack isn't knowing everything; it's understanding how everything connects. In 2026, with AI handling boilerplate and frameworks abstracting complexity, the real leverage is in seeing the whole system and knowing where the actual problems hide.

This essay argues for the generalist approach: why breadth enables better architectural decisions, how context-switching makes you a better problem solver, and why the ability to navigate the entire stack—from database indexes to CSS animations—is more valuable than deep expertise in a framework that might be obsolete in three years. We'll also cover how to go deep as a generalist without spreading yourself too thin.
