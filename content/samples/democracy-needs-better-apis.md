Government data is technically "public," but try accessing it and you'll find PDFs, locked spreadsheets, and systems designed to obscure rather than illuminate. Democracy dies not from a lack of transparency laws, but from a lack of accessible infrastructure. Citizens can't hold institutions accountable if the data requires a FOIA request and a data science degree to parse.

This essay explores why modern democracy needs well-designed APIs as much as it needs well-designed laws. We'll look at successful examples from around the world, the technical and political barriers to government API adoption, and practical steps for turning opacity into accountability. If software is eating the world, it needs to start with making our institutions digestible.
