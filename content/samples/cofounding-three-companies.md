I've co-founded three companies: one in legal tech, one in events platforms, and one in same-day delivery. One was acquired, one pivoted into something unrecognizable, and one died quietly. Each taught me expensive lessons about product-market fit, co-founder dynamics, and the difference between a good idea and a viable business. If I could go back, here's what I'd do differently.

This is a brutally honest retrospective: the partnership mistakes that nearly killed a profitable business, the technical decisions that seemed smart but aged poorly, the market signals we ignored because we were too attached to our vision, and the moments where doubling down would have worked but we blinked. Failure is expensive, but it's even more expensive if you don't learn from it.
