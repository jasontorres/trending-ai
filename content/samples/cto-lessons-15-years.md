After 15 years of leading engineering teams—from 3-person startups to 50+ person orgs—I've learned that the hardest problems in engineering are rarely technical. They're about people, process, and the culture you build when no one's watching. The best CTOs aren't the ones who write the most code; they're the ones who create environments where great work happens naturally.

This is a collection of hard-won lessons: when to refactor vs. rebuild, how to hire when you're competing with FAANG, building teams that don't need you, managing technical debt as a financial instrument, and the difference between systems that scale and systems that collapse under their own complexity. These are the things I wish someone had told me when I first became a technical leader.
