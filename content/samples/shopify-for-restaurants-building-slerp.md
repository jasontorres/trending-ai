Restaurants were getting crushed by marketplace fees—30% commissions that evaporated profit margins while platforms owned the customer relationship. We built Slerp to give restaurants what Shopify gave retailers: full control of their brand, data, and customer relationships. Turns out, building "Shopify for restaurants" meant solving problems Shopify never had to think about—like dispatch logistics, real-time inventory during rush hours, and POS integrations that actually work.

This article covers the technical and business challenges of building a restaurant platform: designing for offline-first in kitchens with spotty WiFi, handling the complexity of modifiers and substitutions, integrating with legacy POS systems, building dispatch orchestration that works across multiple providers, and the unit economics that make or break a platform business. We'll also explore what we got wrong and the features that seemed critical but nobody used.
